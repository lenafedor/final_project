RECIPE

1. 

To make the lecsó, boil the tomatoes, peel and cut into wedges. Wash the peppers, drain, remove the seeds and veins and cut into finger-thick rings.

2. 

Peel and finely dice the onion. Cut the bacon into small cubes.

3. 

Put the bacon on the oil and fry it in the fat over a moderate heat. Fry the onion, remove from the heat, sprinkle with the red pepper, add the tomatoes and season to taste.

4. 

Return to the heat and simmer uncovered for a few minutes.

5. 

Toss in the peppers and simmer, half covered, over a moderate heat for 15 minutes until tender, tasting and adding salt again if needed.

6. 

Serve with fresh bread.

LECSÓ

40 minutes

fry

Lecsó is a traditional Hungarian vegetable stew primarily made with bell peppers, tomatoes, onions, and paprika. It is a popular dish in Hungarian cuisine, known for its vibrant flavors and simplicity. Lecsó can be served as a main dish, side dish, or used as a base for other recipes. Often, it includes additional ingredients like sausages, bacon, or eggs for added richness. The dish is typically seasoned with salt, pepper, and sometimes garlic, reflecting the robust and hearty nature of Hungarian cooking.
