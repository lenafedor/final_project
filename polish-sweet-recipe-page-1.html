RECIPE

1. 

Prepare the Pastry Dough. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt.

2. 

Add the cold, cubed butter and cut it into the flour using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in the sour cream until the dough comes together.

3. 

Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

4. 

Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

5. 

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/8 inch thickness. Cut the dough into long strips about 1 inch wide. Wrap each strip around a metal cream horn mold, starting at the tip and overlapping slightly as you go. Lightly press the end to secure it. Place the wrapped molds on the prepared baking sheet.

6. 

Brush the pastry tubes with the beaten egg and sprinkle with granulated sugar.

7. 

Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly before carefully removing the molds.

8. 

In a medium saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it starts to simmer. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, flour, cornstarch, and egg yolks until smooth. Gradually pour the hot milk into the egg mixture, whisking constantly to prevent curdling. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened and boiling.

9. 

Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Let the custard cool to room temperature, then beat in the softened butter until smooth and creamy.

10. 

Fill the Pastry Tubes. Transfer the cream filling to a piping bag fitted with a large round tip. Pipe the cream into the cooled pastry tubes, filling them generously.

11. 

Serve the filled pastries immediately, or refrigerate until ready to serve.

RURKA Z KREMEM

1,5 hour

cook

200°C

Rurka z Kremem is a traditional Polish pastry known for its delightful combination of crispy pastry and rich, creamy filling. The dessert consists of tubular pastry shells, typically made from a buttery, flaky dough, which are baked until golden brown. Once cooled, the pastry tubes are filled with a sweet and smooth cream, often made from a custard base mixed with butter for added richness. These elegant treats are often enjoyed as a special dessert during holidays and celebrations, cherished for their delicate texture and indulgent flavor.

INGREDIENTS

• 

2 cups all-purpose flour

• 

1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed

• 

1/2 cup sour cream

• 

A pinch of salt

• 

1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

• 

Granulated sugar (for sprinkling)

• 

2 cups whole milk

• 

1/2 cup granulated sugar

• 

4 tablespoons all-purpose flour

• 

2 tablespoons cornstarch

• 

4 egg yolks

• 

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

• 

1/2 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
