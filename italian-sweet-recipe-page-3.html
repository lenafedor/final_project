RECIPE

1. 

First, insert the flour, sugar and vanilla into the bowl of the planetary mixer or mixer, add the dry brewer's yeast, if you are using fresh yeast, dilute it in a teaspoon of water.

2. 

Then mix with the whisk and add the eggs previously beaten together 3 times. Let the previous ones absorb before adding others. The dough will be sticky, this is normal, continue working at medium-high speed until it sticks to the leaf.

3. 

Finally add the soft butter cut into pieces and the salt. It will take about 10 minutes, little by little you will see the dough become more and more elastic and form a veil.

4. 

Transfer to a bowl, don't worry if it sticks to your hands. Cover with cling film and leave to rise for about 4 hours in a dry place at a temperature of 28°C, oven off with light on, until it quadruples in volume.

5. 

Then transfer the dough into a previously buttered pan. Don't worry if it's sticky. If you're using small molds, butter them well and use half the mixture compared to the capacity of the mold.

6. 

Finally let it rise at 28°C until the dough reaches the edge of the mold or just above the edge of the moulds. Then cook in a very hot static oven in the central part at 200°C for the first 10 minutes, then lower to 180°C and continue for another 5 - 8 minutes! Be careful that the toothpick must be dry but must not dry out or burn too much.

7. 

While it is cooking in the oven, prepare the syrup by bringing the water and sugar to the boil. When the sugar has completely dissolved after stirring constantly, turn off the heat and add the rum. Mix and let cool completely to room temperature.

8. 

Finally, when it is ready, take your babà or mini unmoulded versions out of the oven onto a wire rack and leave to cool for at least 2 hours. For the perfect soaking, the leavened product must be cold. First, slowly sprinkle the upper surface and sides, until pay the entire quantity. You will see that it will be immersed in the liquid. Press gently and repeat the operation several times, decanting the liquid and spongeing your babà well so that it is effectively soaked and engaged on all sides, including the central ones. Slice, if you see that it is not very dry, add 2 tablespoons of syrup. Serve at room temperature or chilled accompanied by whipped cream and fruit of your choice!

BABA

8 hours

200°C

The Baba, Babba or Babà al rum is a leavened dessert with a mushroom or scalloped donut shape, typical of the Neapolitan pastry world famous throughout the world, soft and delicate, perfect for concluding a holiday lunch. A dessert so loved by Neapolitans that it has become part of their history.

INGREDIENTS

• 

310 g Manitoba flour

• 

4 dry brewer's yeast (or 12 g of fresh brewer's yeast)

• 

520 g sugar

• 

5 g salt

• 

6 eggs

• 

100 g butter

• 

500 g of water

• 

180 g rum

• 

200 g fresh liquid cream

• 

20 g icing sugar

• 

fruit (to taste)
