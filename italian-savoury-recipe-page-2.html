RECIPE

1. 

Start by pouring the water into a bowl and dissolving the yeast in it. Once melted, pour in a first part of flour, about half, and start kneading with a spoon, using a spoon movement that always brings the dough up and towards the center from below.

2. 

While you are mixing, add the salt. By continuing to gradually add the flour and repeating the movement with the spoon, you will see that the dough will take on a softer and more compact consistency.

3. 

When you have finished all the flour, and the dough no longer leaves any residue on the spoon, let it rest in the bowl, covering it for ten minutes.Continue kneading, and then leave to rest for another twenty minutes.

4. 

Then turn the dough onto a work surface onto which you have poured a little flour. Here you will make the so-called folds: by lifting the dough from the center, you will fold it on itself. Do this three times: this serves to strengthen the gluten mesh. After three folds, try to give the dough a round shape by modeling it with your hands. Finally, put it back in the bowl and cover it with a lid or cling film.

5. 

Put the bowl in the fridge for eight hours. Once the eight hours have passed, take it out of the fridge and begin the staglio phase, i.e. the portioning of the loaves. Calculate 180 grams for each loaf. Then let the dough balls rest in a closed container for another four hours.

6. 

The time has come to roll out the pizzas. Use the slapping technique to spread it further. When the dough is well rolled out, season it to taste.If you use an electric oven at 250 degrees, pre-heat it ten minutes before cooking. Then place the pizza on a baking tray in the lower part. After about seven minutes, move the pizza to the top, as close to the top grill as possible. This way, the cornice will toast a little more, but try not to hold it too much otherwise it will risk burning.

PIZZA A PORTAFOGLIO

14 hours

250°C

The wallet pizza is a small pizza, which takes its name from the fact that it is taken in the hands and closed on itself just as if it were a wallet. In Naples it is sold on the street by pizzerias, bakeries, pastry shops and takeaways: it is probably the symbolic street food of the Neapolitan capital.
