RECIPE

1. 

Heat the Milk. In a large pot, heat the milk to 30°C (86°F), stirring occasionally to prevent scorching.

2. 

Dissolve the rennet in 1/4 cup of water. Add the rennet solution to the warm milk, stirring gently for a few minutes.

3. 

Cover the pot and let the milk sit undisturbed for about 30-45 minutes, or until it sets into a firm curd.

4. 

Cut the Curd. Once the curd is set, cut it into small cubes (about 1 cm or 1/2 inch) using a long knife. Let the curds sit for 5 minutes to firm up slightly.

5. 

Cook the Curd. Slowly heat the curds to 38°C (100°F), stirring gently to prevent them from clumping together. Maintain this temperature for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

6. 

Line a colander with cheesecloth and carefully pour the curds into it to drain the whey

7. 

Gather the corners of the cheesecloth and tie them into a bundle. Hang the bundle over a sink or bowl to drain for about an hour.

8. 

After draining, transfer the curds into a cheese mold or shape them by hand into a spindle shape or small wheels. Press the cheese firmly to expel any remaining whey and to form a solid block. Let it sit in the mold or under pressure for a few hours.

9. 

Remove the cheese from the mold and sprinkle salt evenly over the surface. Rub the salt into the cheese and let it sit for a few hours or overnight.

10. 

Prepare a smoker with wood chips and heat it to a low temperature (around 60-70°C or 140-160°F). Place the cheese in the smoker and smoke it for 2-4 hours, or until it develops a golden-brown color and a distinct smoky flavor.

11. 

Let the smoked cheese cool to room temperature. Wrap it in wax paper or cheesecloth and store it in the refrigerator. Oscypek can be enjoyed fresh or aged for a more intense flavor.

OSCYPEK

2 days

cook

Oscypek is a traditional smoked cheese made from sheep's milk in the Tatra Mountains of Poland. Known for its distinct spindle shape and decorative patterns, oscypek has a firm texture and a salty, smoky flavor. It's often enjoyed sliced, grilled, or paired with cranberry sauce.
