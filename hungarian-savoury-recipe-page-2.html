RECIPE

1. 

Grind the yeast into the lukewarm milk, sprinkle 2 handfuls of flour and the icing sugar on top, mix a little and let it rise.

2. 

Put the rest of the flour in a large mixing bowl and stir in the salt. When the leaven has risen, pour it over the flour and start mixing. Keep pouring in the clarified water as the dough absorbs it. It should be the consistency of flame-grilled dough. Cover the dough in the mixing bowl with a tea towel and leave to rise for 2 hours.

3. 

Then put the dough on a floured kneading board, knead it a little, divide it into 3 parts and leave it to rise for 1 hour.

4. 

While the dough is rising, prepare the topping. Fry the bacon in a frying pan and put the bacon drippings on a plate. Add the diced onion to the fat and fry until translucent, then add the chopped garlic, season with salt and pepper to taste. When well browned, add the sour cream and cook for 1 minute. Preheat the oven to 250°C.

5. 

While the oven is heating, take out a baking tray and place one of the separated dough pieces in the middle of the kneading board, roll out to the size of the tray and about 1,5 cm thick. Place it on the baking tray and pre-bake in the preheated oven for 5 minutes, then remove and pour the sour cream sauce over it, add sausage, ham, bacon and cheese to taste. Place in the oven and bake at 250°C for 5 minutes. Do the same with the other two pieces of dough.

Lángos, also known as langallo, is a popular Hungarian street food consisting of a deep-fried flatbread typically topped with various ingredients. The dough is made from flour, yeast, water, and salt, and after frying, it is traditionally served with toppings such as garlic, sour cream, and grated cheese. Some variations might include additional toppings like ham, sausages, or vegetables. Lángos is enjoyed for its crispy exterior and soft, chewy interior, making it a beloved snack or meal at fairs, markets, and festivals in Hungary.
