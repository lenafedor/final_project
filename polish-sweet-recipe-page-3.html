RECIPE

1. 

Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Line a baking pan (preferably a springform pan) with parchment paper. Grease the sides of the pan.

2. 

In a large bowl, beat the egg yolks and sugar together until the mixture is pale and fluffy. Gradually add the melted butter, mixing continuously until well combined. Stir in the vanilla extract and lemon zest.

3. 

In a separate bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour, potato starch, and baking powder. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the egg yolk mixture, mixing until smooth.

4. 

In another bowl, beat the egg whites with a pinch of salt until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the batter, being careful not to deflate them.

5. 

Pour a thin layer of batter (about 1/4 cup) into the prepared baking pan, spreading it evenly. Place the pan in the oven and bake for about 5-7 minutes, or until the layer is golden brown. Remove the pan from the oven and pour another thin layer of batter over the baked layer.

6. 

Bake again until golden brown. Repeat this process until all the batter is used up, creating multiple layers. Each layer should be thin and evenly baked.

7. 

Once all the layers are baked, allow the cake to cool completely in the pan. Carefully remove the cake from the pan and transfer it to a serving plate. Slice the cake to reveal the beautiful, tree-like layers.

SĘKACZ

40 min

180°C

Sekacz, also known as "Tree Cake," is a traditional Polish-Lithuanian cake known for its distinctive, tree-ring-like layers. It's made by baking thin layers of batter one at a time, resulting in a unique, cylindrical cake with a characteristic ringed appearance. This delicious and visually striking cake is often served at special occasions and enjoyed with tea or coffee.

INGREDIENTS

• 

10 large eggs, separated

• 

1 1/2 cups granulated sugar

• 

1 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled

• 

1 cup all-purpose flour

• 

1 cup potato starch

• 

1 teaspoon baking powder

• 

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

• 

Zest of 1 lemon

• 

A pinch of salt
