RECIPE

1. 

Prepare the Baking Sheet. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

2. 

In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the sugar, water, light corn syrup, cream of tartar, and salt. Stir the mixture over medium heat until the sugar is completely dissolved.

3. 

Add Flavor and Color. Remove the saucepan from the heat. Stir in the vanilla extract and a few drops of red food coloring, if desired, to achieve the classic pink color of panska skorka.

4. 

Carefully pour the hot mixture onto the prepared baking sheet. Allow the mixture to spread out naturally; it should be about 1/4 inch thick. Let it cool until it's firm but still pliable.

5. 

Once the candy is cool enough to handle but not completely hardened, cut it into small rectangular pieces using a knife or scissors. Allow the pieces to cool completely and harden.

6. 

Store the panska skorka pieces in an airtight container at room temperature to keep them fresh.

PAŃSKA SKÓRKA

2 hours

cook

Panska Skorka is a traditional Polish candy, often found at fairs and festivals. It is a type of nougat or taffy-like sweet, typically pink and white in color, with a chewy texture. Known for its nostalgic appeal, Panska Skorka is enjoyed for its simple yet delightful flavor.

INGREDIENTS

• 

2 cups granulated sugar

• 

1 cup water

• 

1/2 cup light corn syrup

• 

1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar

• 

1/4 teaspoon salt

• 

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

• 

Red food coloring (optional)
