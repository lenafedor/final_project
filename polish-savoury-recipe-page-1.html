RECIPE

1. 

Preheat your oven to 200°C (390°F).

2. 

Prepare the Baguette. Cut the baguette in half lengthwise, creating two long pieces. If using a large baguette, you can cut it into smaller portions for easier handling.

3. 

Cook the mushrooms and onions. In a large skillet, heat the butter and olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until they become translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the sliced mushrooms and minced garlic to the skillet. Cook until the mushrooms are soft and any moisture has evaporated, about 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

4. 

Assemble the zapiekanka. Place the baguette halves on a baking sheet, cut side up. Evenly distribute the cooked mushroom and onion mixture over the baguette halves. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese generously over the top.

5. 

Bake. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven. Bake for about 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the edges of the bread are golden brown.

6. 

Finish and serve. Remove the zapiekanka from the oven and let it cool slightly. Drizzle ketchup over the top in a zigzag pattern. Optionally, garnish with chopped fresh parsley. Serve hot, with pickles on the side if desired.

ZAPIEKANKA

40 min

fry

200°C

Zapiekanka is a popular Polish street food, often called Polish pizza. It consists of a halved baguette topped with sautéed mushrooms, onions, and melted cheese, typically mozzarella. The open-faced sandwich is baked until crispy and gooey, then drizzled with ketchup. It's a simple yet flavorful snack, cherished for its versatility and nostalgic charm.

INGREDIENTS

• 

1 large baguette (or 2 small ones)

• 

200g (about 7 oz) mushrooms, sliced

• 

1 small onion, finely chopped

• 

200g (about 7 oz) shredded mozzarella cheese

• 

2 tablespoons butter

• 

2 tablespoons olive oil

• 

1 clove garlic, minced

• 

Salt and pepper to taste

• 

Ketchup (for drizzling)

• 

Fresh parsley, chopped (optional for garnish)

• 

Pickles (optional for serving)
