RECIPE

1. 

To make the strudel, sift the two types of flour into a bowl. Dissolve the salt in the warm water and add the vinegar. The amount of water added is determined by the quality of the flour, because if the flour is of better quality it absorbs more water, if it is of poorer quality it absorbs less.

2. 

Knead the dough and knead it well for about 10-15 minutes. (It is good if the dough is shiny and bubbly and does not stick to your hands or the board). Then cut the dough into four pieces, brush the little buns with oil and put them in a warm place to rest for half an hour. While the dough rests, we prepare the fillings.

3. 

For the sour cherry strudel, gently squeeze the juice from the sour cherries, but do not remove it completely. Add the semolina or breadcrumbs, cinnamon and cloves to the sugar, then mix the whole mixture with the sour cherries. If there is still a lot of juice in the cherries, leave it in the bottom of the pan.

4. 

When the dough has rested long enough, roll it out one at a time. (If you press your finger into the dough and it stays in place, it's quite soft.) To stretch it, place a clean towel on the table, pat it dry, then flatten the dough with your palm and start to pull it out. With your hands, grease the top of the dough slightly, and with the back of your hand, working from the inside out, gently pull it from the bottom until it is almost transparent.

5. 

Once it is pulled out evenly, carefully pinch off the remaining thicker pieces from the edges, or simply cut around them with scissors. (If you have a juicier filling, you can throw these pieces in between the filling.) Let it dry a little, but don't dry it too long, or it will break when rolled up. Before filling, brush thinly with melted fat. This is done for all the bars.

6. 

When the dough is rolled out and the fillings are ready, fill the strudel one by one. Fold the two longer edges in on both sides and then roll up loosely with the spreader. Brush the top with the egg yolk and place on a baking tray lined with silicone baking paper. Bake in a preheated oven at 200°C for 15-20 minutes until red. When baked, slice and serve with the top sprinkled with icing sugar.

RÉTES

1 hour

fry

bake

Rétes, also known as Hungarian strudel, is a traditional Hungarian pastry made with a thin, flaky dough filled with sweet or savory fillings. Common sweet fillings include apple, cherry, poppy seed, or sweet cottage cheese, while savory versions might contain ingredients like cabbage or spinach. The dough is stretched very thin before being filled, rolled up, and baked until golden and crispy. Rétes is enjoyed for its delicate layers and rich, flavorful fillings, making it a popular dessert or snack in Hungarian cuisine.
