RECIPE

1. 

To prepare the cheesy sour cream lángos, warm up the milk. Place the sugar in a glass, crumble the yeast into it, and pour in half of the milk. Cover it and let it rise.

2. 

Put the flour and salt in a bowl and mix them well. Make a well in the center, and add the oil, the risen yeast mixture, the remaining milk, and a little warm water. Start mixing, gradually adding the water. When the dough is moderately soft, knead it thoroughly, place it in a proofing bowl, and let it rise for about 1 hour.

3. 

After that, oil a tray and, with oiled hands, tear off small portions of the dough, placing them on the tray slightly apart from each other. Cover them with a kitchen towel and let them rise further. When the dough has nicely risen (about 30 minutes), you can start frying.

4. 

Pour plenty of oil into a pan and heat it well. Then, using oiled hands, stretch and shape the dough before placing it into the hot oil. Fry both sides to a nice golden brown in about 1 minute per side. Drain the excess oil by placing the fried dough on paper towels.

5. 

To serve, sprinkle with salt, spread with sour cream, and sprinkle with grated cheese. If desired, you can also spread it with garlic.

LÁNGOS

2 hours

fry

A staple in Hungarian street food, Langos is a deep-fried flatbread traditionally topped with sour cream and grated cheese. This golden, crispy delight can also be found with a variety of toppings, from garlic butter and smoked sausage to modern twists like Nutella and fresh fruits. Whether you prefer savory or sweet, Langos is a must-try treat that embodies the warmth and hospitality of Hungarian cuisine.
