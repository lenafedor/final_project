RECIPE

1. 

Sift the flour into a bowl, make a well in the middle and rub in the yeast. Add the warm milk and stir to dissolve the yeast in the milk. Cover and allow to rise for 5-10 minutes.

2. 

Mix the butter, sugar and eggs well, add to the flour and knead the dough by hand until smooth. Shape into a loaf, sprinkle with a little flour and cover and knead for 30 minutes until doubled.

3. 

Roll out the dough on a floured work surface and cut 2 cm wide strips by hand. Butter a calacatta baking tree and roll the strips up on the tree.

4. 

Sprinkle a tray with granulated sugar and roll the dough in it before baking. Bake over charcoal, turning slowly, for about 10-15 minutes.

5. 

It is ready when the sugar caramelises on the dough, at which point it is rolled in different flavoured sugars.

KÜRTŐSKALÁCS

40 min

200°C

Kürtőskalács, also known as chimney cake, is a traditional Hungarian pastry made from sweet, yeast dough. The dough is rolled into a long strip, wrapped around a cylindrical baking spit, and then baked over an open flame or in a rotisserie oven. During baking, the pastry is brushed with butter and often sprinkled with sugar, which caramelizes to form a crispy, golden crust. Once baked, Kürtőskalács can be rolled in various toppings such as cinnamon, nuts, cocoa, or coconut. The pastry is known for its unique spiral shape and delightful combination of a crispy exterior and soft, fluffy interior. It is a popular treat at fairs, festivals, and Christmas markets in Hungary.
