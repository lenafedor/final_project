RECIPE

1. 

Prepare the Smalec. In a medium skillet, melt the pork lard over low to medium heat.

2. 

Add the finely chopped onion and cook until it becomes translucent and starts to caramelize, about 5-7 minutes.

3. 

Add the finely chopped apple and minced garlic. Cook, stirring frequently, until the apple pieces are soft and have blended well with the onions, about 5 minutes.

4. 

Stir in the marjoram, and season with salt and pepper to taste. If you're using bits of crispy bacon or pork cracklings, stir them in at this point.

5. 

Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, ensuring everything is well combined and heated through.

6. 

Cool the Smalec. Remove the skillet from heat and allow the smalec mixture to cool slightly.

7. 

Transfer the mixture into a container or a bowl and let it cool completely. Once cooled, refrigerate for a few hours or until the smalec becomes firm.

8. 

Spread the chilled smalec generously over slices of fresh rye bread. Serve with pickles on the side for a traditional touch.

CHLEB ZE SMALCEM

30 min

fry

Chleb ze Smalcem, or "bread with lard," is a traditional Polish snack. It is made by spreading rendered pork lard, often mixed with caramelized onions, apples, and spices, onto slices of crusty rye bread. It's typically served with pickles on the side, offering a savory and hearty treat that's deeply rooted in Polish culinary traditions.

INGREDIENTS

• 

250g (about 9 oz) pork lard

• 

1 small apple, peeled, cored, and finely chopped

• 

1 small onion, finely chopped

• 

1 clove garlic, minced

• 

1 teaspoon marjoram

• 

Salt and pepper to taste

• 

Optional: bits of crispy bacon or pork cracklings

• 

Fresh rye bread or any crusty bread of your choice

• 

Pickles (such as dill pickles or gherkins)
