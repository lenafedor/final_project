RECIPE

1. 

Start by preparing the pre-dough, with 100 g of flour taken from the total, mixed with the yeast, 100 g of water taken from the total, 1 teaspoon of sugar. Cover and let rise at 28°C for about 1 hour and a half, long enough for all the bubbles to form and for it to double in volume.

2. 

Then add the flour to the yeast, the sugar, milk, mix with the blade arm of the planetary mixer to mix together and add the aromas (finely grated orange and lemon peel, vanilla and honey).

3. 

Continue kneading at medium speed. Add the eggs one at a time. When the first egg is absorbed, add the second. Finally add the soft butter in small pieces or the oil in teaspoons.

4. 

Add the salt, continue stringing, making folds by hand if necessary. The final dough must be soft and smooth.

5. 

At this point you can add the raisins, previously softened in cold water, perfectly squeezed and dry.

6. 

Work the dough on the table, giving it the shape of a nice homogeneous ball which you leave to rise in a greased bowl inside. Cover with cling film and let the Maritozzi dough rise until it triples in volume (about 3 - 4 hours).

7. 

Divide the dough into small loaves of 80 g each, roll them on the work surface and give them a slightly oval shape. Place all the rolls obtained on a large baking tray lined with baking paper. Cover with cling film and let them rise for an hour in the oven.

8. 

When the Maritozzi have almost doubled their volume, brush with egg yolk: cook in a static oven in the medium-low part of the oven at 180°C for about 25 minutes until golden.

9. 

Once cooked, let them cool completely, then with the help of a knife cut the Maritozzi with an oblique cut and fill them with lots of fresh cream.

10. 

Just before serving them, prepare the water and sugar syrup for the glazing.Place approximately 1 glass of water with 3 tablespoons of sugar on the heat and cook over a low heat. when the sugar has dissolved, brush the Maritozzi and sprinkle them with icing sugar.

MARITOZZO

5 hours

180°C

Maritozzo is a delicious traditional Roman recipe: these are very soft leavened dough rolls with an intense aroma given by the aromas used within the dough, cut in half and overflowing with fresh whipped cream. They are among the most exquisite desserts of the Roman confectionery tradition, perfect for breakfast accompanied by a nice cappuccino, or for a sweet snack.

INGREDIENTS

• 

500 g of Manitoba flour

• 

1 teaspoon of dry brewer's yeast (or 8 g of fresh brewer's yeast)

• 

100g of water

• 

150 g of whole milk

• 

120 g of sugar + 3 tablespoons for icing

• 

2 eggs + 1 yolk for brushing

• 

90 g of butter (or 90 g sunflower seed oil)

• 

1 tablespoon honey

• 

Grated peel of 1 lemon

• 

Grated peel of 1 orange

• 

Vanilla

• 

8 g of salt

• 

40 g of raisins (optional)

• 

300 ml of whipped cream for filling

• 

Icing sugar for dusting
